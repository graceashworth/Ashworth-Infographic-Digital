Step One

Online Behavior Is Harvested Into Raw Data

Companies are always watching you. Every click, swipe, like, reaction, and upload leaves behind a digital footprint. Platforms collect this "data exhaust" invisibly through cookies, tracking pixels, device fingerprinting, and app permissions buried inside terms of service and privacy policies that confuse and overwhelm users into blindly consenting.

Shoshana Zuboff calls this practice Surveillance Capitalism: the online human experience itself becomes raw material for profit, used to train algorithms that manipulate, target, and follow you wherever you click.

"Surveillance capitalism unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data."

- Shoshana Zuboff, The Age of Surveillance Capitalism (2019)

Step Two

Turning Data Into Power

Raw data on its own isn't the product - but it's transformed into something far more powerful: predictive behavioral profiles. Algorithms are trained with your data to analyze and infer your thoughts, fears, desires, insecurities, and political beliefs.

Researchers Benkler, Faris, and Roberts (2018) document that Facebook collects highly detailed data about its users and transforms it into microtargeted content designed to influence and persuade them into certain thoughts and beliefs.

"Describing this business as 'advertising' or 'behavioral marketing' rather than 'microtargeted manipulation' makes it seem less controversial."

- Benkler, Faris and Roberts, Network Propaganda (2018)
Embedded Manipulation: Digital interfaces also serve as surveillance infrastructure. Every design choice on platforms is engineered not for your benefit, but to maximize the amount of data you generate.
Attention Engineering

Infinite Scroll

Removing natural stopping points keeps you on-platform indefinitely, generating more behavioral data with every swipe.

Dopamine Loop

Variable Reward Alerts

Unpredictable notifications mimic slot machine mechanics, creating compulsive checking behavior.

Emotion Exploitation

Outrage Amplification

Algorithms boost content triggering fear and anger because emotionally activated users stay on platforms longer.

Dark Pattern

Buried Consent

Privacy settings are hidden deep in menus. Data collection is the default. Opting out is deliberately difficult.

Step Three

Fighting Back

Digital literacy means understanding how the system works well enough to limit your exposure, make informed choices, and advocate for structural change. While you can't delete your digital footprint entirely, awareness is key.

Audit your app permissions

Revoke location, microphone, and contact access from apps that don't need it. If an app is free and you can't explain why, you're paying with data.

Use a tracker blocker

Extensions like Privacy Badger block third-party trackers that follow you across the web and feed data broker pipelines.

Adjust privacy settings

Most apps let you limit data collection or targeted advertising. Reviewing these settings regularly reduces your exposure.

Read before you agree

Use tosdr.org to quickly see what a platform's privacy policy actually says in plain language.

Recognize manipulation tactics

Knowing about infinite scroll, outrage bait, and variable reward notifications makes them less effective on you.

Demand regulation

The U.S. has no comprehensive federal data privacy law. The EU's GDPR demonstrates regulation is possible. Contact your representatives.
